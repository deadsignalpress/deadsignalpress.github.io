Four Horror Stories of Decay, Madness, and the Supernatural

Delve into the Decay

Dead Signal Press is proud to present a collection that festers at the crossroads of body horror and psychological terror. Rot, Water, and Other Infections is a visceral journey into the things that break down—from the wood of a forgotten house, to the sanity of a lonely mind, to the very flesh on our bones.

This debut collection features four chilling novellas:

  • 1. The Rot Beneath In the deep South of Mississippi, a woman inherits her grandmother’s crumbling home only to discover that something festers beneath the floorboards. What begins as a foul odor soon becomes a grotesque infestation that spreads into her body, mind, and soul. Some inheritances are better left untouched.
  • 2. Don’t Drink the Water In South Central Los Angeles, a teenage girl realizes the people she loves are changing — her friends, her neighbors, even her own family. Their eyes are dull, their skin ripples, their words drip with something inhuman. All of it started with the tap water. To survive, she must decide whether to resist the infection — or let it consume her.
  • 3. The Thing You Brought Back Curiosity drives a young woman into a forgotten part of Los Angeles where urban legends whisper of a place that swallows people whole. She goes looking for a story but finds something else instead — something that follows her back, seeping into her life, her friends, and her dreams. Some stories should never be told.
  • 4. Scrape Me Clean A Black lesbian in her 40s struggles with personal trauma and broken memories — but her body begins betraying her in grotesque ways. Her skin peels, her muscles tear, and something inside claws to be free. In a desperate attempt to purge the infection, she spirals into madness, discovering that some wounds can never be scraped clean.

For readers of Thomas Ligotti, Carmen Maria Machado, and Brian Evenson, this collection will linger in your mind long after the final page… and make you wary of the dark, damp corners of your world.
